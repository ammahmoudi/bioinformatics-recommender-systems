Why restart?

Without restart, the walker may drift far away and recommend generally popular nodes. Restart keeps the score personalized around the starting user, disease, or drug.

6. Graph neural networks

Nodes update by receiving messages.

A molecule is a graph: atoms are nodes and bonds are edges. A GNN aggregates neighbor messages to predict toxicity, binding, or side effects.

Message Passing

Carbon atom
Oxygen atom
Updated oxygen vector
Toxicity prediction
\( H^{(l+1)} = \sigma(\tilde{D}^{-\frac{1}{2}} \tilde{A} \tilde{D}^{-\frac{1}{2}} H^{(l)} W^{(l)}) \)

Self-looped adjacency

\(\tilde{A} = A + I\). Normal graph edges plus each node's edge to itself, so every node keeps its own current features while receiving neighbor messages.

Tools

PinSage-style graph recommenders, GraphSAGE, LightGCN, PyTorch Geometric, DGL, StellarGraph, Decagon, DeepChem, DGL-LifeSci, Chemprop, RDKit-backed pipelines.

7. Reinforcement learning

Generate actions, score rewards, improve policy.

In movies, the action is showing a recommendation and the reward is engagement. In de novo drug design, the action adds an atom or ring and the reward combines binding, toxicity, and QED.
